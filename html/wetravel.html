We Travel

A Messaging and UX Audit

Overview

WeTravel is an online trip planner and payment platform targeted towards semi-professionals, boutique agencies and DMCs (Destination Management Companies).

The Challenge

There are two main challenges to address: How to pivot focus from the marketplace to their payment platform feature and how to provide the right messaging to target the different groups that use their site?

My Role

Working on a team with three other UX designers, we were all closely involved in the end to end process of this project.

Skills

User Research | Wireframing | Card Sorting | Task Flow | Prototyping | Lo/Hi-Fidelity Design | Sketch

Outcome

Through our changes, we were able to get 100% of users to correctly identify the target market and were successful in getting 60% of users to correctly calculate the pricing for WeTravel. We made the pricing more transparent and easy to understand and also improved clarity on the landing page. By the end of our cycles, we were able to increase user engagement and clicks on the call to action button.


The Process


1. Research Phase



Our research phase consisted of user interviews, affinity mapping, card sorting, comprehension tests and competitor messaging analysis.

From our research, we were able to determine the top three things users felt were important:

  • Manage Participant Info
  • Collect Money Safely
  • Create and Share Itinerary

2. Experimentation Phase

During our experimentation phase, we started with testing the control, which is the original and current WeTravel website. Using the control as the basis for our changes, we began four cycles:

For every cycle, we tested 5 new target users. With each cycle, we used the results from the previous one to create a new hypothesis in hopes of reaching our goal.

Landing Page

Our goal for the landing page was to widen the target market to also include professionals such as DMC companies and Boutique Tour Agencies. Currently, the original WeTravel site appeals more towards semi-professionals (i.e. Yoga and MBA Students).

Control

We started with testing the original WeTravel page so that we would have a control to compare our results to.

During the user interviews, we asked them to answer a simple question, "Who do you think this site is for?"

Results

Only 20% were able to correctly identify the target market.

  • Users were overwhelmed by the amount of site content
  • Users thought the site was for individual travlers and were distracted by the "Discover Trips" links
  • Users thought the site was solely for yoga instructors

Cycle 1

Hypothesis

If we focus messaging to match user values, then 80% of users will be able to identify the target market correctly.

Summary of Changes

Results

100% of users correctly identified the target market!

  • Users were able to correctly identify the target market
  • Users overlooked the video on the page mistaking it for a photo
  • Users did not pay attention to the three main features at the bottom of the page
Cycle 1 Prototype

Cycle 2

Because we were successful in getting users to correctly identify the target market, we created a new goal. Our client wanted us to increase clicks to the pricing page.

Hypothesis

If we emphasize the “How it Works” section, then 80% of users will feel confident enough to click on the pricing page

Summary of Changes

Results

Only 20% of users were ready to click on the pricing page from the landing page.

  • Users still expressed a need to know more before they felt ready to learn about pricing or to create a trip
  • Users did not react well to the hero image and text
Cycle 2 Prototype

Cycle 3

Hypothesis

If we de-emphasize “How it Works” and emphasize “Pricing”, 80% of users will want to go to the pricing page.

Summary of Changes

Results

40% of users wanted to go to the pricing page as their first click

  • Users saw the features but didn't seem convinced on how they could benefit from WeTravel
  • Users understood how it works, but were not sure if they would use the service
Cycle 3 Prototype

Cycle 4

Hypothesis

If we emphasize user benefits, 80% of users will want to click on the pricing page.

Summary of Changes

Results

60% of users wanted to go to the pricing page as their first click!

  • Users wanted to watch the "How it Works" video and then click on pricing
  • Users felt confident that it was easy to get more information
Cycle 4 Prototype

Takeaways

Although we did not reach our goal of 80% in the final cycle, we still felt success because the remaining 20% voiced that they wanted to go to the "How it Works" page first and review the pricing page afterwards.
